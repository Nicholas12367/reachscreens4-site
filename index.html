The Problem

You've Already Tried
the Obvious Channels.

Most local business owners we talk to have already spent on the usual suspects — and felt the budget disappear without much to show for it. Here's why.

Social ads reach everyone — and no one.

A Facebook or Instagram ad gets 1–3 seconds of attention as the user scrolls past. You're paying for thumb-flicks, not customers, and your ad disappears into a feed competing with friends and family.

Print and flyers feel local but can't be measured.

Newspaper and direct-mail feel like the right channel for a local business. But you can't tell who saw it, the design fee eats the budget, and most copies end up in the recycling bin.

Billboards are built for highways, not neighbourhoods.

A big roadside billboard costs more than most local budgets allow, and it's built to be glanced at by drivers at 100 km/h — not seen by the customer who's about to make a buying decision in your part of town.

None of those are bad channels. They're just not built for a local business advertising to local people. What you actually need is your ad in front of customers where they already are, while they're already paying attention.

The Solution

We've placed indoor digital screens inside the businesses Lloydminster locals visit every day — restaurants, salons, gyms, coffee shops, waiting rooms, retail floors. Your ad plays where attention is already paid.

Repeated Exposure
